# The Vectors

## When Your Mind's Made Up

You know you've got me tied up in your chains
My eyes light up at the sound of your name
But it feels like I'm being ignored
And it feels like, I've been here before

I wish you'd just look in my direction
Show me any kind of affection
But my calls out to her
Always go unreturned
I'm always left unanswered

There she goes again
Tear my heart open
This feels so familiar
There she goes with him
All that could have been
Guess I'm starting over

So when your mind's made up
Can you please let me know?
Because I'm tired of waiting out here all alone.
Thought she was great, thought she was lovely
But all that time on my mind
She wasn't thinking of me

## Disconnected / Discontented

You turned to me and said, we gotta keep moving, before our roots grow too deep
This place is starting to look too much like places we’ve already seen
So we packed our bags and took off again
In search of a place that we could fit in
Without the fear that someone’d remember our names

Disconnected, never enough
Discontented, where is the love?
Where do you go when your youth is used up?
Is there more to life than giving up?

## Rock N’ Roll Laundromat

Once a week, I grab my detergent and all my dirty clothes
And I pack them up and walk down the road
I divide them into color groups and gather all my coins
I’m doing laundry today, oh boy!

But what’s this? I call the guys in my band
We set up a stage in the laundromat
It’s time to rock n’ roll while my clothes get clean
Rock N’ Roll laundromat, I wish I could wash clothes everyday

It wasn't long before we pissed off the proprietor
He said it was the worst music he’d ever heard, absurd!
So we turned it up real loud and threw his ass outside
Pretty soon we had a crowd, this is the best day of my life

But what’s this? I have a plan. We climb onto the roof and there we jam
What a day at the laundromat. Washing clothes and rocking rolls. 

## The Curse Of The Floss

It’s Halloween again on Mulberry street
The favorite place for kids to trick or treat
The best free candy in all the land
Chocolates and gummies and all the name brands
But in a boring house at the end of the block
Lives a grumpy old dentist who only gives out floss
Dr. Jarvis hated cavities and Halloween fun
He wanted all trick-or-treaters to have healthy gums

The kids decided they couldn’t take it anymore
So they made a plan for when they knocked on his door
Instead of taking the floss and walking away
They tied him up so he would feel their pain
And they made him eat all kind of sugary treats
Until Dr. Jarvis, forgot his fear of cavities
And then he bought candy for all of the kids
To make up for all of the flossing he did
